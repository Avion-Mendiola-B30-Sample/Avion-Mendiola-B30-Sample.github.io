What is Google?

Google is a search engine that was registered on 15th September 1997 and the company got incorporated on 4th September 1998. The founders of Google, Larry and Sergey invented Google's PageRank algorithm together. They initially named Google “BackRub” as the system checked backlinks to estimate the importance of a site. The founders of Google later changed the name to Google, inspired by the word “Googol.” This name was picked to signify that the search engine was initiated to provide information in huge quantities.

About Larry Page

Larry Page stepped down as CEO of Alphabet, the parent company of Google, in 2019 but remains a board member and a controlling shareholder. Page was CEO until 2001, when Eric Schmidt took over, and then from 2011 until 2015, when he became CEO of Google's new parent firm Alphabet. With Brin, Page invented Google's PageRank algorithm, which powers the search engine.

About Sergey Brin

Brin moved to the U.S. from Russia when he was 6 years old in the wake of anti-Semitism against his family. Sergey Brin stepped down as president of Alphabet, parent company of Google, in December 2019 but remains a board member and a controlling shareholder. Brin is reportedly funding a high-tech airship project.
